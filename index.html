Mission Statement

At Binary Dreams, we believe in the power of technology to dramatically change people’s lives. We have watched as the Internet enabled people from around the globe to connect with one another, to exchange ideas, and to expand their minds. Technology has the power to bring people together, and to make the world a more accessible place.

People with disabilities can, with the help of technology, begin to interact with the world in ways that were not previously possible. For example, consider Stephen Hawking’s wheelchair. With the help of this device, Hawking was able to travel the world, give lectures, and publish groundbreaking researching. All of this despite severe mobility issues.

Our co-founder, Mauricio Zelaya, has a child with autism. Through his experience with his son, and through years of working with care providers, he has noticed several things. First, many people with autism are more comfortable interacting with the world through computers or smart phones. In addition, this population struggles with fine motor skills. This need is what led to Binary Dreams. By taking advantage of the technology that we carry with us every day, we have developed a stimulating practice tool that fits in one’s pocket.

Using our application, people with autism or who have other mobility issues can engage their fine motor skills in a fun and challenging environment. Harnessing the power of a phone’s gyroscope and accelerometer, we can target the movements of the wrists and hands to improve fine motor control.

We know that both parents and occupational therapists will see the value in this application. Here at Binary Dreams, we are Making the World a Better Place, One Bit at a Time.
